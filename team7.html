Dr. Murali Krishna Gumma

Dr. Murali Krishna Gumma is, currently, a Scientist - GIS/Geospatial science and leading RS/GIS team with the International Crops Research Institute for the Semi-Arid Tropics (ICRISAT) working across Asia and Africa. His recent work on South Asian Chickpea mapping and prioritization of water sheds in Ethiopia at ICRISAT is well recognized.

  • Gumma, M.K, Thenkabail, P.S, Andrew, N, Maunahan, A, Islam, S. (2014), Mapping seasonal rice cropland extent and area in the high cropping intensity environment of Bangladesh using MODIS 500 m data for the year 2010. ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing. (press)
  • Gumma, M.K, Mohanty, S, Andrew, N, Rala, A, Irshad, A.M, Das, S.R. (2014) Remote sensing based change analysis of rice environments in Odisha, India. Journal of Environmental Management. (Press).
  • Gumma, M.K, and Pavelic, P. (2013). Identifying groundwater potential zones across Ghana using Remote sensing techniques. Environmental monitoring and Assessment. 185 (4), 3561-3579.
  • Gumma, M.K, Andrew, N., Thenkabail, P.S. and A.N.Singh.. (2011). Mapping rice areas in South Asia using MODIS multi temporal data. Journal of Applied Remote Sensing, Vol 5, 053547 (Sep 01, 2011); doi:10.1117/1.361983.
  • Gumma, M.K, Gauchan, D, Andrew, N., Pandey, S. and Rala, A. (2011).Temporal changes in rice-growing area and their impact on livelihood over a decade: a case study of Nepal. Agriculture, Ecosystems & Environment.142 (3-4), 382-392.

Prior to ICRISAT, he was Remote Sensing Scientist with the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI) working across Asia. Prior to this he was a post-doctoral researcher at IRRI. His recent work on South Asian rice mapping (Gumma et al., 2011) at IRRI is well recognized and was funded by the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation.
 Before IRRI, he was special project scientist and a Remote Sensing Officer (regional) at the International Water Management Institute (IWMI) and was based in India and Sri Lanka but worked across Asia and Africa. Murali made significant contribution to irrigated area mapping (http://www.iwmidsp.org). This is borne out by his outstanding publications:

  • Gumma, M.K., Thenkabail, P.S., Muralikrishna, I.V., Velpuri, M.N., Gangadhararao, P.T., Dheeravath, V., Biradar, C.M., Acharya Nalan, S., Gaur, A., 2011. Changes in agricultural cropland areas between a water-surplus year and a water-deficit year impacting food security, determined using MODIS 250 m time-series data and spectral matching techniques, in the Krishna River basin (India). International Journal of Remote Sensing 32(12), 3495-3520.
  • Gumma, M.K., Thenkabail, P.S., 2011. Methods and Approaches of irrigated area mapping using Remote Sensing. LAP LAMBERT Academic Publishing GmbH & Co. KG Dudweiler Landstr. 99, 66123 Saarbrücken, Germany. Schaltungsdienst Lange o.H.G., Berlin Books on Demand GmbH, Norderstedt Reha GmbH, Saarbrücken Amazon Distribution GmbH, Leipzig.   • Gumma, M.K., Thenkabail, P.S., Fujii, H., Nelson, A., Dheeravath, V. Rala, A., and Busia. D. 2011. Mapping Irrigated Areas of Ghana Using Fusion of 30 m and 250 m Resolution Remote-Sensing Data.  Journal Remote Sensing. 2011, 3(4), 816-835.

 He made significant contribution to a paper that won the 2008 John I. Davidson ASPRS President’s Award for practical papers (second place):

  • Thenkabail, P.S., GangadharaRao, P., Biggs, T., Krishna, M., and Turral, H., 2007. Spectral Matching Techniques to Determine Historical Land use/Land cover (LULC) and Irrigated Areas using Time-series AVHRR Pathfinder Datasets in the Krishna River Basin, India. Photogrammetric Engineering and Remote Sensing. 73(9): 1029-1040. (Second Place Recipients of the 2008 John I. Davidson ASPRS President’s Award for Practical papers).

 Gumma has 15+ years of extensive experience working as a leading remote sensing and geographic information systems (RS/GIS) and their application to agriculture, water, wetlands, and natural resource management. His work experience spans 20 Countries spread across South Asia, Central Asia, South East Asia, West Africa and East Africa. He has done extensive field campaigns to gather cropland and water data in these Countries. He is the author of 36 peer-reviewed scientific papers and 1 academic book. In 2009, Dr. Gumma got second best PhD dissertation award from the Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University (JNTU), Hyderabad, India.
