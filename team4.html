Dr. Cristina Milesi

Cristina Milesi is a senior research scientist with California State University Monterey Bay and a member of the Ecological Forecasting Group at NASA Ames Research Center since 2005.

Her research applies remote sensing and ecosystem modeling to the study of the interactions between climate and land use changes and their impacts on the current and projected functioning of ecosystems. She is particularly interested in applying satellite data to monitoring agricultural productivity as impacted by climate and technological changes, and has studied the effects of irrigation and ground water exploitation on the dynamics of agricultural production in the semi-arid tropics. Her interests also include understanding urban land use changes and quantifying the environmental consequences of urban expansion on vegetation productivity and landscape water use. She is engaged in defining data requirements and helping communities develop adaptations strategies to climate variability and change and is a member of the NASA Climate Science Adaptation Investigators team. Dr. Milesi is involved with the NASA Earth Exchange, a collaborative research sharing, supercomputing platform for Earth science, where she catalyzes the user community.

Prior to joining California State University Monterey Bay, Dr. Milesi has been a NASA Earth System Science Fellow (2000-2003) and a National Research Council Fellow (2004). She has been a research collaborator with the Department of Biology of the University of Trieste and the Centre of Theoretical and Applied Ecology, Italy, from 1994 to 1998, where she has worked on land cover mapping and biomass estimation of agricultural and forested areas. She has consulted with the United Nations Industrial Development Organization to produce instructional GIS material. She received an undergraduate degree in Biology from the University of Trieste (1995), where she graduated magna cum laude, and a PhD in ecosystem modeling and remote sensing from the University of Montana (2004).
