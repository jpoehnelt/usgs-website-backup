Ms Michela Marinelli
Geospatial Analyst Expert
FAO

Michela Marinelli

Ms Michela Marinelli is an Environmental Engineer working since 2004 at the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) as geospatial analyst expert. She mainly focused on climate impact assessment, water resource assessment and spatial analysis for agriculture water management. She recently joined the FAO Statistics Division (ESS) to contribute to the establishment of a Regional Collaborative Platform to monitor crop water productivity and drought among the Countries of the NENA Region, in the framework of the Regional Water Scarcity Initiative (WSI). As a member of the team of the FAO Global Information and Early Warning System on Food and Agriculture (GIEWS) and, later on, of the Land and Water division, she could build a good knowledge of the agro-climatic and crop yield forecasting subjects and she could work with the most relevant FAO databases (AQUASTAT, FAOSTAT, GAEZ, etc.) and applications (AquaCrop, CropWat, MOSAICC and Agrometshell). Furthermore she was in charge of the design and implementation of models and tools based on remotely sensed data (SPOT Vegetation, MODIS, Landsat, etc.) for predicting and monitoring drought effects on agriculture.

She contributed to the following documents/publications:

  • FAO Irrigation & Drainage paper 66: Crop Yield Response to Water (FAO.2012)
  • The State of the World's Land and Water resources for food and agriculture(SOLAW) (FAO.2011)
  • Suitability Assessment of Bioenergy Crops: the case study of Tanzania (FAO.2010)
  • The Global Administrative Unit Layers, Technical Aspects (FAO.2008)
  • Food Insecurity, Poverty and Environment Global GIS Database (FGGD) and Digital Atlas for the Year 2000 (FAO.2006)
